<p><p>The recycled thermoplastic market is divided into biodegradable and non-biodegradable types. Biodegradable thermoplastics are derived from renewable resources and can decompose naturally, minimizing environmental impact after disposal. They are often used in packaging and agricultural applications. In contrast, non-biodegradable thermoplastics are made from fossil fuels and do not break down easily, leading to long-term waste issues. These are commonly utilized in automotive, construction, and consumer goods, highlighting diverse applications but raising concerns about sustainability and environmental pollution.</p></p>
