<p><p>EVA (ethylene-vinyl acetate) packaging lining is increasingly utilized for its flexibility and durability. In the injection moulding segment, molten EVA is injected into a mold to create precise, complex shapes, ideal for high-volume production of packaging components. Meanwhile, compression moulding involves placing EVA material into a heated mold, where it is then compressed to form various packaging shapes. Both methods cater to different requirements, with injection moulding offering higher detail and compression moulding providing efficiency for simpler designs.</p></p>
