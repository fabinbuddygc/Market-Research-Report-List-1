<p><ul><li>SLC</li><li>MLC</li><li>TLC</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Solid-state drives (SSDs) are categorized based on their memory cell architecture: SLC (Single-Level Cell), MLC (Multi-Level Cell), and TLC (Triple-Level Cell). SLC stores one bit per cell, offering superior performance and endurance, making it ideal for enterprise applications. MLC holds two bits per cell, balancing cost and performance for consumer use. TLC stores three bits per cell, providing higher capacity at a lower cost, suitable for general storage needs, but with reduced speed and lifespan compared to SLC and MLC.</p></p>
