<p><ul><li>Active TB</li><li>Latent TB</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The tuberculosis (TB) treatment market is divided into two main types: Active TB and Latent TB. Active TB treatment involves antibiotics prescribed for individuals showing symptoms or confirmed infection, typically requiring a rigorous, multi-drug regimen lasting several months. In contrast, Latent TB treatment targets asymptomatic individuals who carry the bacteria but do not exhibit disease signs, often employing shorter antibiotic courses to prevent progression to active TB. This segmentation allows tailored strategies to manage and reduce TB incidence effectively.</p></p>
