<p><ul><li>Sawtooth Modulation</li><li>Triangular Modulation</li><li>Square-wave Modulation</li><li>Stepped Modulation</li><li>Sinusoidal Modulation</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Frequency-Modulated Continuous-Wave (FMCW) Radar is a technology used for distance measurement and target detection by analyzing frequency variations over time. The market includes various modulation types: </p><p>1. **Sawtooth Modulation**: Characterized by a linear frequency ramp, ideal for smooth distance measurement. </p><p>2. **Triangular Modulation**: Similar to sawtooth, but features a symmetrical ramp up and down, enhancing detection accuracy. </p><p>3. **Square-wave Modulation**: Provides distinct frequency shifts, beneficial for high-resolution applications. </p><p>4. **Stepped Modulation**: Features discrete frequency changes, suitable for multi-target detection. </p><p>5. **Sinusoidal Modulation**: Offers continuous frequency variation, enhancing signal quality over longer distances.</p></p>
